White's Turn

Github Repo: github.com/DylanSOldham/ChessButWithMoreDimensions

This is chess, except after move 6 you have the option to use your turn to arbitrarily connect two board squares instead of moving a piece. The board starts with horizontal (Dimension 1) and vertical (Dimension 2) connections already in place. You are free to add connections of dimensions from 1 to 5, as long as they don't land on a square that's in the sight of an opposing piece. The visual layout of all of the squares on the screen does not matter, only the connections between them.

There is no AI opponent - it's a hotseat game.

You can move the pieces like normal by left-clicking and dragging.

You can rearrange the board squares themselves by right-clicking and dragging them to a new location.

To add a new connection after the 6th move, click on the button that says "Add Connection", use the adjacent selector to choose a dimension, and then left-click a beginning and an end square to create the connection.

One major note though: Try to avoid making loops. The game doesn't like it very much.
